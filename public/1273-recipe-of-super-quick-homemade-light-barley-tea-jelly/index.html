The ingredients needed to make Light Barley Tea Jelly:
  1. Make ready 800 ml Water
  2. Take 10 grams Gelatin
  3. Get 6 tbsp Sugar
  4. Take 15 grams Barley tea
  5. Take 1 Kinako and milk

In Japan, people called it Mugicha. It is very popular in China, Korea, Japan, and America. Some people drink it instead of coffee. Although barley tea is regarded as a summer beverage by many Japanese, I drink it regularly all year round, after a bath.

Instructions to make Light Barley Tea Jelly:
  1. 15 g of barley tea leaves is about this much; about 4 tablespoons.
  2. Bring water to a boil in a pot that can be made air-tight.
  3. Turn off the heat and add the tea leaves all at once.
  4. Once the tea leaves are added, close the lid and steam for 1:30 minutes.
  5. Once steamed, line a strainer with a paper towel and strain the tea. It should become this dark.
  6. Turn on the heat again and dissolve the sugar and gelatin into the tea while warming it. Once dissolved, transfer to a heatproof container and chill in the refrigerator.
  7. Transfer your desired amount to a dish with a spoon. Top as you like with kinako and milk to make it like a Japanese-style coffee jelly.
